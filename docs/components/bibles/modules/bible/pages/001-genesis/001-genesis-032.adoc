[[v1-32-0]]
=== Genesis, 32
// nwt 2013, nwt - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures

[[v1-32-1]]

^<<v1-32-1,1>>^ Jacob then went on his way, and the angels of God met up with him.

[[v1-32-2]]

^<<v1-32-2,2>>^ As soon as he saw them, Jacob said: “This is the camp of God!” So he named that place Ma·ha·naʹim.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Meaning “Two Camps.”
====

[[v1-32-3]]

^<<v1-32-3,3>>^ Then Jacob sent messengers ahead of him to his brother Eʹsau in the land of Seʹir, the territory^&#42;1^ of Eʹdom,

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “field.”
====

[[v1-32-4]]

^<<v1-32-4,4>>^ and he commanded them: “This is what you will say to my lord, to Eʹsau, ‘This is what your servant Jacob says: “I have resided^&#42;1^ with Laʹban for a long time until now.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “resided as a foreigner.”
====

[[v1-32-5]]

^<<v1-32-5,5>>^ And I have acquired bulls, donkeys, sheep, and male and female servants, and I send this message to inform my lord, in order to find favor in your eyes.”’”

[[v1-32-6]]

^<<v1-32-6,6>>^ In time the messengers returned to Jacob, saying: “We met your brother Eʹsau, and he is now on his way to meet you, and there are 400 men with him.”

[[v1-32-7]]

^<<v1-32-7,7>>^ And Jacob became very frightened and anxious. So he divided the people who were with him, as well as the flocks, the cattle, and the camels, into two camps.

[[v1-32-8]]

^<<v1-32-8,8>>^ He said: “If Eʹsau attacks the one camp, then the other camp will be able to escape.”

[[v1-32-9]]

^<<v1-32-9,9>>^ After that Jacob said: “O God of my father Abraham and God of my father Isaac, O Jehovah, you who are saying to me, ‘Return to your land and to your relatives, and I will deal well with you,’

[[v1-32-10]]

^<<v1-32-10,10>>^ I am unworthy of all the loyal love and of all the faithfulness that you have shown toward your servant, for with only my staff I crossed this Jordan and now I have become two camps.

[[v1-32-11]]

^<<v1-32-11,11>>^ Save me, I pray you, from the hand of my brother Eʹsau, for I am afraid of him that he may come and attack me, as well as the mothers and their children.

[[v1-32-12]]

^<<v1-32-12,12>>^ And you have said: ‘I will certainly deal well with you, and I will make your offspring^&#42;1^ like the grains of sand of the sea, which are too numerous to count.’”

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “seed.”
====

[[v1-32-13]]

^<<v1-32-13,13>>^ And he spent the night there. Then he took some of his possessions as a gift for Eʹsau his brother:

[[v1-32-14]]

^<<v1-32-14,14>>^ 200 female goats, 20 male goats, 200 female sheep, 20 rams,

[[v1-32-15]]

^<<v1-32-15,15>>^ 30 camels nursing their young, 40 cows, 10 bulls, 20 female donkeys and 10 full-grown male donkeys.

[[v1-32-16]]

^<<v1-32-16,16>>^ He handed them over to his servants, one drove after another, and he said to his servants: “Cross over ahead of me, and you are to set a space between one drove and the next.”

[[v1-32-17]]

^<<v1-32-17,17>>^ He also commanded the first one: “In case Eʹsau my brother should meet you and ask, ‘To whom do you belong, and where are you going, and to whom do these ahead of you belong?’

[[v1-32-18]]

^<<v1-32-18,18>>^ then you should say, ‘To your servant Jacob. It is a gift sent to my lord, to Eʹsau, and look! he himself is also behind us.’”

[[v1-32-19]]

^<<v1-32-19,19>>^ And he commanded also the second, the third, and all those following the droves: “According to this word, you are to speak to Eʹsau when you meet him.

[[v1-32-20]]

^<<v1-32-20,20>>^ And you should also say, ‘Here is your servant Jacob behind us.’” For he said to himself: ‘If I appease him by sending a gift ahead of me, then afterward when I see him, he may give me a kindly reception.’

[[v1-32-21]]

^<<v1-32-21,21>>^ So the gift crossed over ahead of him, but he himself spent the night in the camp.

[[v1-32-22]]

^<<v1-32-22,22>>^ Later during that night, he rose and took his two wives and his two female servants and his 11 young sons and crossed over the ford of Jabʹbok.

[[v1-32-23]]

^<<v1-32-23,23>>^ So he took them and brought them across the stream,^&#42;1^ and he brought over everything else he had.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “torrent valley; wadi.”
====

[[v1-32-24]]

^<<v1-32-24,24>>^ Finally Jacob was left by himself. Then a man began to wrestle with him until the dawn broke.

[[v1-32-25]]

^<<v1-32-25,25>>^ When he saw that he had not prevailed over him, he touched the socket of his hip; and the socket of Jacob’s hip was dislocated during his wrestling with him.

[[v1-32-26]]

^<<v1-32-26,26>>^ After that he said: “Let me go, for the dawn is breaking.” To this he said: “I am not going to let you go until you bless me.”

[[v1-32-27]]

^<<v1-32-27,27>>^ So he said to him: “What is your name?” to which he said: “Jacob.”

[[v1-32-28]]

^<<v1-32-28,28>>^ Then he said: “Your name will no longer be Jacob but Israel,^&#42;1^ for you have contended with God and with men and you have at last prevailed.”

[NOTE]
====
. Meaning “Contender (Perseverer) With God” or “God Contends.”
====

[[v1-32-29]]

^<<v1-32-29,29>>^ In turn Jacob inquired: “Tell me, please, your name.” However, he said: “Why is it that you ask my name?” With that he blessed him there.

[[v1-32-30]]

^<<v1-32-30,30>>^ So Jacob named the place Pe·niʹel,^&#42;1^ for he said, “I have seen God face-to-face, yet my life^&#42;2^ was preserved.”

[NOTE]
====
. Meaning “Face of God.”
. Or “soul.”
====

[[v1-32-31]]

^<<v1-32-31,31>>^ And the sun rose upon him as soon as he passed by Pe·nuʹel,^&#42;1^ but he was limping because of his hip.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “Peniel.”
====

[[v1-32-32]]

^<<v1-32-32,32>>^ That is why to this day the sons of Israel are not accustomed to eat the thigh sinew,^&#42;1^ which is on the socket of the hip joint, because he touched the socket of Jacob’s hip joint by the thigh sinew.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “the sinew of the thigh nerve.”
====
