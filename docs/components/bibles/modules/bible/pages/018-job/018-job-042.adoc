[[v18-42-0]]
=== Job, 42
// nwt 2013, nwt - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures

[[v18-42-1]]

^<<v18-42-1,1>>^ Then Job said in reply to Jehovah:

[[v18-42-2]]

^<<v18-42-2,2>>^ “Now I know that you are able to do all things
And that nothing you have in mind to do is impossible for you.

[[v18-42-3]]

^<<v18-42-3,3>>^ You said, ‘Who is this who is obscuring my counsel without knowledge?’
Therefore I spoke, but without understanding
About things too wonderful for me, which I do not know.

[[v18-42-4]]

^<<v18-42-4,4>>^ You said, ‘Please listen, and I will speak.
I will question you, and you inform me.’

[[v18-42-5]]

^<<v18-42-5,5>>^ My ears have heard about you,
But now I do see you with my eyes.

[[v18-42-6]]

^<<v18-42-6,6>>^ That is why I take back what I said,^&#42;1^
And I repent in dust and ashes.”

[NOTE]
====
. Or “make a retraction.”
====

[[v18-42-7]]

^<<v18-42-7,7>>^ After Jehovah had spoken these words to Job, Jehovah said to Elʹi·phaz the Teʹman·ite:
“My anger burns against you and your two companions, for you have not spoken the truth about me as my servant Job has.

[[v18-42-8]]

^<<v18-42-8,8>>^ Now take seven bulls and seven rams and go to my servant Job, and offer up a burnt sacrifice for yourselves. And my servant Job will pray for you. I will surely accept his request^&#42;1^ not to deal with you according to your foolishness, for you have not spoken the truth about me as my servant Job has.”

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “Surely I will lift up his face.”
====

[[v18-42-9]]

^<<v18-42-9,9>>^ So Elʹi·phaz the Teʹman·ite, Bilʹdad the Shuʹhite, and Zoʹphar the Naʹa·ma·thite went and did what Jehovah had told them to do. And Jehovah accepted Job’s prayer.

[[v18-42-10]]

^<<v18-42-10,10>>^ After Job had prayed for his companions, Jehovah removed Job’s tribulation and restored his prosperity.^&#42;1^ Jehovah gave him double what he had before.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “Jehovah turned back the captivity of Job.”
====

[[v18-42-11]]

^<<v18-42-11,11>>^ All his brothers and sisters and all his former friends came to him and ate a meal with him in his house. They sympathized with him and comforted him over all the calamity that Jehovah had allowed to come upon him. Each of them gave him a piece of money and a gold ring.

[[v18-42-12]]

^<<v18-42-12,12>>^ So Jehovah blessed the last part of Job’s life more than the beginning, and Job came to have 14,000 sheep, 6,000 camels, 1,000 pairs of cattle, and 1,000 female donkeys.

[[v18-42-13]]

^<<v18-42-13,13>>^ He also came to have seven more sons and three more daughters.

[[v18-42-14]]

^<<v18-42-14,14>>^ He named the first daughter Je·miʹmah, the second Ke·ziʹah, and the third Kerʹen-hapʹpuch.

[[v18-42-15]]

^<<v18-42-15,15>>^ No women in all the land were as beautiful as Job’s daughters, and their father gave them an inheritance along with their brothers.

[[v18-42-16]]

^<<v18-42-16,16>>^ After this Job lived for 140 years, and he saw his children and his grandchildren—four generations.

[[v18-42-17]]

^<<v18-42-17,17>>^ Finally Job died, after a long and satisfying life.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “old and full of days.”
====
