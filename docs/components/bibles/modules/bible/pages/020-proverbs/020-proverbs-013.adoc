[[v20-13-0]]
=== Proverbs, 13
// nwt 2013, nwt - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures

[[v20-13-1]]

^<<v20-13-1,1>>^ A wise son accepts his father’s discipline,
But the scoffer does not listen to a rebuke.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or “correction.”
====

[[v20-13-2]]

^<<v20-13-2,2>>^ From the fruitage of his speech^&#42;1^ a man will eat what is good,
But the very desire^&#42;2^ of the treacherous is for violence.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “mouth.”
. Or “soul.”
====

[[v20-13-3]]

^<<v20-13-3,3>>^ The one guarding his mouth^&#42;1^ protects his life,^&#42;2^
But the one opening his lips wide will come to ruin.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “what he says.”
. Or “soul.”
====

[[v20-13-4]]

^<<v20-13-4,4>>^ The lazy person has his cravings, yet he^&#42;1^ has nothing,
But the diligent one^&#42;2^ will be fully satisfied.^&#42;3^

[NOTE]
====
. Or “his soul.”
. Or “soul.”
. Lit., “will be made fat.”
====

[[v20-13-5]]

^<<v20-13-5,5>>^ The righteous one hates lies,
But the actions of the wicked bring shame and disgrace.

[[v20-13-6]]

^<<v20-13-6,6>>^ Righteousness protects the one whose way is innocent,
But wickedness brings down the sinner.

[[v20-13-7]]

^<<v20-13-7,7>>^ There is one who pretends to be rich, yet has nothing;
There is another who pretends to be poor, yet has great wealth.

[[v20-13-8]]

^<<v20-13-8,8>>^ Riches are the ransom for a man’s life,^&#42;1^
But the poor are not even threatened.^&#42;2^

[NOTE]
====
. Or “soul.”
. Lit., “hear no rebuke.”
====

[[v20-13-9]]

^<<v20-13-9,9>>^ The light of the righteous shines brightly,^&#42;1^
But the lamp of the wicked will be extinguished.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “rejoices.”
====

[[v20-13-10]]

^<<v20-13-10,10>>^ Presumptuousness leads only to strife,
But wisdom belongs to those who seek advice.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or “those consulting together.”
====

[[v20-13-11]]

^<<v20-13-11,11>>^ Wealth quickly gained^&#42;1^ will dwindle,
But the wealth of the one who gathers it little by little^&#42;2^ will increase.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “Wealth from vanity.”
. Lit., “gathers by hand.”
====

[[v20-13-12]]

^<<v20-13-12,12>>^ Expectation^&#42;1^ postponed makes the heart sick,
But a desire realized is a tree of life.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “Hope.”
====

[[v20-13-13]]

^<<v20-13-13,13>>^ Whoever despises instruction^&#42;1^ will pay the penalty,
But the one who respects the commandment will be rewarded.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “the word.”
====

[[v20-13-14]]

^<<v20-13-14,14>>^ The teaching^&#42;1^ of the wise one is a source of life
To turn one away from the snares of death.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “law.”
====

[[v20-13-15]]

^<<v20-13-15,15>>^ Keen insight wins favor,
But the way of the treacherous is harsh.

[[v20-13-16]]

^<<v20-13-16,16>>^ The shrewd person acts with knowledge,
But the fool exposes his own foolishness.

[[v20-13-17]]

^<<v20-13-17,17>>^ A wicked messenger falls into trouble,
But a faithful envoy brings healing.

[[v20-13-18]]

^<<v20-13-18,18>>^ Whoever neglects discipline comes to poverty and disgrace,
But the one accepting correction^&#42;1^ will be glorified.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “reproof.”
====

[[v20-13-19]]

^<<v20-13-19,19>>^ Desire when realized is sweet to a person,^&#42;1^
But the stupid hate to turn away from bad.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “soul.”
====

[[v20-13-20]]

^<<v20-13-20,20>>^ The one walking with the wise will become wise,
But the one who has dealings with the stupid will fare badly.

[[v20-13-21]]

^<<v20-13-21,21>>^ Calamity pursues sinners,
But prosperity rewards the righteous.

[[v20-13-22]]

^<<v20-13-22,22>>^ The good person leaves an inheritance to his grandchildren,
But the sinner’s wealth will be stored up for the righteous one.

[[v20-13-23]]

^<<v20-13-23,23>>^ The plowed field of the poor yields much food,
But it^&#42;1^ may be swept away by injustice.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “he.”
====

[[v20-13-24]]

^<<v20-13-24,24>>^ Whoever holds back his rod^&#42;1^ hates his son,
But the one who loves him disciplines him diligently.^&#42;2^

[NOTE]
====
. Or “discipline; punishment.”
. Or possibly, “promptly.”
====

[[v20-13-25]]

^<<v20-13-25,25>>^ The righteous one eats and satisfies his appetite,^&#42;1^
But the stomach of the wicked is empty.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “soul.”
====
