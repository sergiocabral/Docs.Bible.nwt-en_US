[[v22-4-0]]
=== The Song of Solomon, 4
// nwt 2013, nwt - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures

[[v22-4-1]]

^<<v22-4-1,1>>^ “Look! You are beautiful, my beloved.
Look! You are beautiful.
Your eyes are those of doves behind your veil.
Your hair is like a flock of goats
Streaming down the mountains of Gilʹe·ad.

[[v22-4-2]]

^<<v22-4-2,2>>^ Your teeth are like a flock of newly shorn sheep
That have come up from being washed,
All of them bearing twins,
And not one has lost her young.

[[v22-4-3]]

^<<v22-4-3,3>>^ Your lips are like a scarlet thread,
And your speech is delightful.
Like a segment of pomegranate
Are your cheeks^&#42;1^ behind your veil.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “temples.”
====

[[v22-4-4]]

^<<v22-4-4,4>>^ Your neck is like the tower of David,
Built with courses of stone
Upon which are hung a thousand shields,
All the circular shields of the mighty men.

[[v22-4-5]]

^<<v22-4-5,5>>^ Your two breasts are like two fawns,
The twins of a gazelle,
That feed among the lilies.”

[[v22-4-6]]

^<<v22-4-6,6>>^ “Until the day grows breezy^&#42;1^ and the shadows flee,
I will go my way to the mountain of myrrh
And to the hill of frankincense.”

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “the day breathes.”
====

[[v22-4-7]]

^<<v22-4-7,7>>^ “You are altogether beautiful, my beloved,
There is no blemish in you.

[[v22-4-8]]

^<<v22-4-8,8>>^ Come with me from Lebʹa·non, my bride,
Come with me from Lebʹa·non.
Descend from the peak of A·maʹnah,^&#42;1^
From the peak of Seʹnir, the peak of Herʹmon,
From the lairs of lions, from the mountains of leopards.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “Anti-Lebanon.”
====

[[v22-4-9]]

^<<v22-4-9,9>>^ You have captured my heart, my sister, my bride,
You have captured my heart with one glance of your eyes,
With one pendant of your necklace.

[[v22-4-10]]

^<<v22-4-10,10>>^ How beautiful your expressions of affection are, my sister, my bride!
Your expressions of affection are far better than wine,
And the fragrance of your perfume than any spice!

[[v22-4-11]]

^<<v22-4-11,11>>^ Your lips, my bride, drip with comb honey.
Honey and milk are under your tongue,
And the fragrance of your garments is like the fragrance of Lebʹa·non.

[[v22-4-12]]

^<<v22-4-12,12>>^ My sister, my bride, is like a locked garden,
A locked garden, a spring sealed shut.

[[v22-4-13]]

^<<v22-4-13,13>>^ Your shoots^&#42;1^ are a paradise^&#42;2^ of pomegranates
With the choicest fruits, with henna along with spikenard plants,

[NOTE]
====
. Or possibly, “skin.”
. Or “garden.”
====

[[v22-4-14]]

^<<v22-4-14,14>>^ Spikenard and saffron, cane^&#42;1^ and cinnamon,
With all sorts of trees of frankincense, myrrh, and aloes,
Along with all the finest perfumes.

[NOTE]
====
. An aromatic reed.
====

[[v22-4-15]]

^<<v22-4-15,15>>^ You are a garden spring, a well of fresh water,
And flowing streams from Lebʹa·non.

[[v22-4-16]]

^<<v22-4-16,16>>^ Awake, O north wind;
Come in, O south wind.
Breathe^&#42;1^ upon my garden.
Let its fragrance spread.”
“Let my dear one come into his garden
And eat its choicest fruits.”

[NOTE]
====
. Or “Blow gently.”
====
