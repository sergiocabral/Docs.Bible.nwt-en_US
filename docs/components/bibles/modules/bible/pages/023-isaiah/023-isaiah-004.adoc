[[v23-4-0]]
=== Isaiah, 4
// nwt 2013, nwt - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures

[[v23-4-1]]

^<<v23-4-1,1>>^ And seven women will grab hold of one man in that day, saying:
“We will eat our own bread
And wear our own clothing;
Only let us be called by your name
To take away our disgrace.”^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. That is, the humiliation of being unmarried and childless.
====

[[v23-4-2]]

^<<v23-4-2,2>>^ In that day what Jehovah makes sprout will be splendid and glorious, and the fruitage of the land will be the pride and beauty of the survivors of Israel.

[[v23-4-3]]

^<<v23-4-3,3>>^ Whoever remains in Zion and is left over in Jerusalem will be called holy, all of those in Jerusalem written down for life.

[[v23-4-4]]

^<<v23-4-4,4>>^ When Jehovah washes away the filth^&#42;1^ of the daughters of Zion and rinses away the bloodshed of Jerusalem from her midst by the spirit of judgment and by a spirit of burning,^&#42;2^

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “excrement.”
. Or “purging.”
====

[[v23-4-5]]

^<<v23-4-5,5>>^ Jehovah will also create over the whole site of Mount Zion and over the place of her conventions a cloud and smoke by day and a bright flaming fire by night; for over all the glory there will be a shelter.

[[v23-4-6]]

^<<v23-4-6,6>>^ And there will be a booth for shade by day from the heat, and for refuge and protection from storms and the rain.
