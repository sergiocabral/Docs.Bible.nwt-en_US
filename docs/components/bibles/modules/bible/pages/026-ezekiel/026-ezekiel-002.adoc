[[v26-2-0]]
=== Ezekiel, 2
// nwt 2013, nwt - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures

[[v26-2-1]]

^<<v26-2-1,1>>^ He then said to me: “Son of man,^&#42;1^ stand up on your feet that I may speak with you.”

[NOTE]
====
. “Son of man”; the first of 93 occurrences of this expression in Ezekiel.
====

[[v26-2-2]]

^<<v26-2-2,2>>^ When he spoke to me, spirit came into me and made me stand up on my feet so that I could hear the One speaking to me.

[[v26-2-3]]

^<<v26-2-3,3>>^ He went on to say to me: “Son of man, I am sending you to the people of Israel, to rebellious nations that have rebelled against me. They and their forefathers have transgressed against me down to this very day.

[[v26-2-4]]

^<<v26-2-4,4>>^ I am sending you to sons who are defiant^&#42;1^ and hardhearted, and you must say to them, ‘This is what the Sovereign Lord Jehovah says.’

[NOTE]
====
. Or “hard of face.”
====

[[v26-2-5]]

^<<v26-2-5,5>>^ As for them, whether they listen or refuse to listen—for they are a rebellious house—they will certainly know that a prophet was among them.

[[v26-2-6]]

^<<v26-2-6,6>>^ “But you, son of man, do not be afraid of them, and do not be afraid of their words, although you are surrounded by briars and thorns^&#42;1^ and are dwelling among scorpions. Do not be afraid of their words, and do not be terrified by their faces, for they are a rebellious house.

[NOTE]
====
. Or possibly, “although the people are obstinate and are like things pricking you.”
====

[[v26-2-7]]

^<<v26-2-7,7>>^ You must speak my words to them, whether they listen or not, for they are a rebellious people.

[[v26-2-8]]

^<<v26-2-8,8>>^ “But you, son of man, listen to what I am telling you. Do not become rebellious like this rebellious house. Open your mouth and eat what I am giving you.”

[[v26-2-9]]

^<<v26-2-9,9>>^ When I looked, I saw a hand stretched out to me, and in it I saw a written scroll.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or “a scroll of a book.”
====

[[v26-2-10]]

^<<v26-2-10,10>>^ When he spread it out before me, it had writing on both front and back. Dirges^&#42;1^ and mourning and wailing were written on it.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “Songs of mourning.”
====
