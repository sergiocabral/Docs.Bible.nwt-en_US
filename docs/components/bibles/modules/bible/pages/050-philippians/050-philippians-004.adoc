[[v50-4-0]]
=== To the Philippians, 4
// nwt 2013, nwt - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures

[[v50-4-1]]

^<<v50-4-1,1>>^ Consequently, my brothers whom I love and long for, my joy and crown, stand firm in this way in the Lord, my beloved ones.

[[v50-4-2]]

^<<v50-4-2,2>>^ I urge Eu·oʹdi·a and I urge Synʹty·che to be of the same mind in the Lord.

[[v50-4-3]]

^<<v50-4-3,3>>^ Yes, I request you also, as a true fellow worker,^&#42;1^ to keep assisting these women who have striven^&#42;2^ side by side with me for the good news, along with Clement as well as the rest of my fellow workers, whose names are in the book of life.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “genuine yokefellow.”
. Or “who have struggled hard.”
====

[[v50-4-4]]

^<<v50-4-4,4>>^ Always rejoice in the Lord. Again I will say, Rejoice!

[[v50-4-5]]

^<<v50-4-5,5>>^ Let your reasonableness become known to all men. The Lord is near.

[[v50-4-6]]

^<<v50-4-6,6>>^ Do not be anxious over anything, but in everything by prayer and supplication along with thanksgiving, let your petitions be made known to God;

[[v50-4-7]]

^<<v50-4-7,7>>^ and the peace of God that surpasses all understanding will guard your hearts and your mental powers^&#42;1^ by means of Christ Jesus.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “your minds; your thoughts.”
====

[[v50-4-8]]

^<<v50-4-8,8>>^ Finally, brothers, whatever things are true, whatever things are of serious concern, whatever things are righteous, whatever things are chaste,^&#42;1^ whatever things are lovable, whatever things are well-spoken-of, whatever things are virtuous, and whatever things are praiseworthy, continue considering^&#42;2^ these things.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “pure.”
. Or “thinking about; meditating on.”
====

[[v50-4-9]]

^<<v50-4-9,9>>^ The things that you learned as well as accepted and heard and saw in connection with me, practice these, and the God of peace will be with you.

[[v50-4-10]]

^<<v50-4-10,10>>^ I rejoice greatly in the Lord that now at last you have renewed your concern for me. Though you were concerned about me, you lacked opportunity to show it.

[[v50-4-11]]

^<<v50-4-11,11>>^ Not that I am saying this because I am in need, for I have learned to be self-sufficient^&#42;1^ regardless of my circumstances.

[NOTE]
====
. Or “content.”
====

[[v50-4-12]]

^<<v50-4-12,12>>^ I know how to be low on provisions and how to have an abundance. In everything and in all circumstances I have learned the secret of both how to be full and how to hunger, both how to have an abundance and how to do without.

[[v50-4-13]]

^<<v50-4-13,13>>^ For all things I have the strength through the one who gives me power.

[[v50-4-14]]

^<<v50-4-14,14>>^ Nevertheless, you did well to share with me in my tribulation.

[[v50-4-15]]

^<<v50-4-15,15>>^ In fact, you Phi·lipʹpi·ans also know that after you first learned the good news, when I departed from Mac·e·doʹni·a, not a congregation shared with me in the matter of giving and receiving, except you alone;

[[v50-4-16]]

^<<v50-4-16,16>>^ for while I was in Thes·sa·lo·niʹca, you sent something to me for my need not just once but twice.

[[v50-4-17]]

^<<v50-4-17,17>>^ Not that I am looking for a gift, but I want the fruitage that brings more credit to your account.

[[v50-4-18]]

^<<v50-4-18,18>>^ However, I have everything I need and even more. I am fully supplied, now that I have received from E·paph·ro·diʹtus what you sent, a sweet fragrance, an acceptable sacrifice, well-pleasing to God.

[[v50-4-19]]

^<<v50-4-19,19>>^ In turn my God will fully supply all your need according to his riches in glory by means of Christ Jesus.

[[v50-4-20]]

^<<v50-4-20,20>>^ Now to our God and Father be the glory forever and ever. Amen.

[[v50-4-21]]

^<<v50-4-21,21>>^ Give my greetings to every holy one in union with Christ Jesus. The brothers who are with me send you their greetings.

[[v50-4-22]]

^<<v50-4-22,22>>^ All the holy ones, but especially those of the household of Caesar, send you their greetings.

[[v50-4-23]]

^<<v50-4-23,23>>^ The undeserved kindness of the Lord Jesus Christ be with the spirit you show.
